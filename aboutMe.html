About Me:

Though my initial training is not in the tech world, I believe my diverse and varied background gives me a uniquely creative viewpoint on tackling software development challenges. Attending both a music highschool for piano performance and then later graduating with a degree in electronic music composition from New College of Florida, my early professional career was spent producing and performing Electronic Dance Music. After living in LA for many years in pursuit of this dream, I achieved many lifelong goals including performing at major music festivals (such as Coachell and EDC Vegas), having my music released on a major record label (Big Beat, Atlantic Records) and having my music licensed to a major television series (The Ozarks). After some time, my desire to take on new and unfamiliar challenges drove me to follow my other passion: brewing. After working in several breweries up and down the west coast from Trustworthy Brewing in Burbank, to MacLeod Ales ub Van Nyes, to Reuben's Brews in Seattle, I eventually ended up at Modern Times in Portland. However, after almost 6 years of brewing, my interests have yet again driven me to take on a new challenge and I find myself drawn to the software development world. Though these worlds I have been in seem different on the outside, they are all linked with the dynamic balance of art and science. Each of my careers have required a skillset of both pragmatic engineering skills, counterpointed with the idealistic dreams of an artist. I hope to apply this style of thinking as I move forward with my career as a software developer.
